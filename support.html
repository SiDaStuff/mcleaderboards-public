Frequently Asked Questions

How do I link my Minecraft account?

Go to your dashboard and complete the onboarding process. You'll be guided through linking your Minecraft account to your MC Leaderboards profile. You'll need to join one of our verification servers and run the /link command with your verification code.

How do I apply for Tier Tester?

Visit your dashboard and click on the "Think you have what it takes? Apply for the tier tester role" banner at the top. Fill out the application form with your details, including your experience level and availability.

How does the Elo rating system work?

The Elo rating system matches you with testers who have similar ratings (Â±600 points). You compete in matches, and your rating adjusts based on match outcomes. Wins against higher-rated opponents give more points, while losses to lower-rated opponents cost more points.

What are the match formats for each gamemode?

Each gamemode has a different match format:

  • Sword: First to 6
  • Axe: First to 10
  • NethOP: First to 3
  • Pot: First to 5
  • SMP: First to 2
  • UHC: First to 4
  • Vanilla: First to 3
  • Mace: First to 3

Vanilla totem drain rule: if both participants are above 1700 Vanilla rating, the drain is 8 totems. Otherwise, it is 14 totems.

What is a provisional rating?

A provisional rating appears when you've played fewer than 10 matches in a gamemode. These ratings are less reliable and may change more significantly as you play more matches. After 10+ matches, the provisional indicator disappears.

How does the new queue system work?

There is now one shared queue for both regular players and Tier Testers.

Every queue entry includes selected gamemodes, selected regions, and a server IP. The system only creates a match when two queued users share at least one gamemode and one region.

At least one of the two queued users must have the Tier Tester role. If only one queued user is a Tier Tester, they become the tester automatically and the other user becomes the player.

If both queued users are Tier Testers, the system still uses the same shared queue, but it randomly assigns one of them as the tester and the other as the player. The match page explains why that assignment happened.

Among all compatible pairs, the system prefers the closest Elo ratings so matches stay as fair as possible.

How do I join the testing queue?

Go to your dashboard, select one or more gamemodes, select one or more regions, enter a whitelisted server IP, and click "Join Queue".

The system places you into the shared queue and looks for the closest compatible pair that shares a gamemode and region with you. A match only starts when at least one queued user in the pair is a Tier Tester.

Why was I removed from the queue?

If no compatible Tier Tester is queued within 5 minutes, you'll be automatically removed from the queue. You can rejoin at any time.

There is also a 30-minute cooldown after completing a match in each gamemode, unless your account has tester/admin queue exemptions.

What are the testing rules?

Match formats and rule details are organized by gamemode on the Rules page. Check both Overall rules and your specific gamemode tab before queueing.

My stats aren't updating. What should I do?

Stats should update automatically after finalized matches. A provisional marker may appear until 10 matches in a gamemode, but ratings and match history still update before that.

If something still looks wrong, refresh once and submit a support form report with the match ID.

How do I change my skill level?

Skill levels can only be set once during onboarding. Once set, they cannot be changed to ensure fair matchmaking. Your Elo rating will adjust automatically based on your match performance.

How do I report a bug or issue?

Use the support form to report bugs or issues. Include as much detail as possible: what happened, when it happened, how to reproduce it, and screenshots if available.

What happens if I disconnect during a match?

If you disconnect, you should rejoin as quickly as possible. Leaving for 15+ minutes without prior notice counts as an automatic loss. Be reasonably understanding of unpreventable circumstances, but communicate with your opponent if possible.

Can I change my linked Minecraft username?

Once linked and verified, your Minecraft username is permanently locked to prevent account spoofing. Contact an administrator if you need to change it due to a username change or other legitimate reason.
